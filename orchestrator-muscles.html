Stage 1

Stabilize & Observe

Survival → Self-awareness

These muscles reduce noise, create psychological breathing room, and help people see how their mind and body are actually operating before we ask them to change anything.

1. The Mindwide Reset
Meta-Cognition Reset · “Executive Prompt Reset”

Teaches people to notice the “prompt” their brain is running and rewrite it toward agency.

Human Classic exercise ›
  • Recall a recent moment of friction or looping self-talk.
  • Write down the exact “internal prompt” your mind was answering (e.g., “Why am I always behind?”).
  • Rewrite that prompt into one that assumes agency (e.g., “What is the next smallest step that moves this forward?”).
  • Say or think the new prompt deliberately, then list 1–3 actions it suggests.
AI-Integrated Coach collaborates ›

Use your AI Coach as a prompt designer:

Executive Prompt Reset: Old mental prompt: "..." New intentional prompt: "..." Help me: - Remove any self-blame or fatalism - Clarify what I'm actually asking for - Propose 1–3 very small actions aligned with the new prompt

The Coach:

  • Strips out self-attack and vague language.
  • Clarifies what “success” might look like.
  • Offers a tiny, doable action list.
AI-Directed Configure your Coach ›

Frame it as: “Teach your AI how your mind currently prompts itself.”

  • Describe one moment where your self-talk was loud.
  • Give the Coach the old prompt and your new, preferred prompt.
  • Ask the Coach: “If you notice me using the old pattern in future sessions, gently nudge me back to the new one.”

The learner believes they’re configuring the AI, but they’re also consciously reconfiguring their own mental default.

2. The Signal–Noise Splitter
Two-Track Mind · Thought vs Feeling

Separates the thinking track and the feeling track so decisions don’t get hijacked by raw emotion.

Human Classic exercise ›
  • Write a brief description of a current issue.
  • On the left: “Thinking Track” – what you believe is true, what you predict will happen.
  • On the right: “Feeling Track” – emotions, body sensations, urges.
  • Ask: “Which track needs attention first for me to move sanely?”
AI-Integrated Coach collaborates ›
Two-Track Mind: Thinking Track: "..." Feeling Track: "..." Coach: - Which track seems most volatile? - Which small action would calm that track without ignoring it? - Once calmer, what decision becomes clearer?

The Coach helps you avoid both “logic-only” and “emotion-only” decisions by sequencing the work.

AI-Directed Configure your Coach ›

Frame it as: “Give your AI data on how your thoughts and feelings diverge.”

  • Describe one situation and fill in both tracks.
  • Ask the Coach: “When my Feeling Track looks like this, what is the safest way you can respond so I don’t feel dismissed?”
  • Optionally: “When my Thinking Track looks like this, how can you gently challenge me without escalating my emotions?”

This trains both the AI and the human in emotional containment.

3. The State Anchor Protocol
90-Second State Shift

A quick physiological reset to keep the nervous system from running the meeting, the project, or the day.

Human Classic exercise ›
  • Name your current state in one word (“amped”, “foggy”, “anxious”).
  • Notice 3 body sensations (temperature, tightness, movement).
  • Do 4 slow breaths with long exhales while feeling your feet or the chair.
  • Choose a single word for the state you want to move toward (“steady”, “curious”, “focused”).
AI-Integrated Coach collaborates ›
State Anchor Protocol: Current state (1–2 words): "..." Body signals: "..." Desired state: "..." Coach: - Normalize what I'm feeling - Identify what this state might be trying to protect - Suggest one 60–120 second micro-action that moves me toward my desired state

The Coach becomes a calm reflector, not an amplifier.

AI-Directed Configure your Coach ›

Frame it as: “Show your AI the shape of your emotional state so it can calibrate.”

  • Describe 1–2 common states you enter at work (e.g., pre-meeting anxiety, end-of-day depletion).
  • Tell the Coach what kind of response helps and what doesn’t when you’re in that state.
  • Ask it to mirror that preference in future interactions.

You’re quietly teaching the AI and yourself how to de-escalate.

Stage 2

Reframe & Play

Awareness → Sandbox

Here we help people loosen the grip of doom-heavy survival narratives and rediscover experimental, sandbox-style thinking.

4. The Hidden Answer Switch
Proxy-Knowing Technique

Bypasses “I don’t know” to access the intuitive answer that was already there.

Human Classic exercise ›
  • Pick a decision or stuck point.
  • Say or write: “I know I don’t know, but if I DID know the next right move, what would it be?”
  • Write the first answer without editing.
  • Later, evaluate that answer with a calmer mind.
AI-Integrated Coach collaborates ›
Hidden Answer Switch: Stuck question: "..." My proxy answer (first thought): "..." Coach: - What looks wise about this answer? - What might be driven by fear, habit, or ego? - Suggest 1–2 safe experiments to test or refine this answer.

Now the Coach helps separate intuition from defense.

AI-Directed Configure your Coach ›

Frame it as: “Upload your intuitive answer so your AI can learn your implicit wisdom.”

  • Share a few past examples where your “if I did know” answer turned out helpful.
  • Ask the Coach to describe what your wise answers have in common.
  • Invite it to remind you of those patterns when you start overthinking.
5. The Narrative Recode
Second Story Method

Turns “I’m doomed” narratives into “I have options” narratives without pretending everything is fine.

Human Classic exercise ›
  • Write down your current story about a situation (First Story).
  • Write a Second Story that is still honest but more empowered and curious.
  • Ask: “What would I do differently if the Second Story were true?”
AI-Integrated Coach collaborates ›
Narrative Recode: First Story: "..." Second Story (my attempt): "..." Coach: - Make my Second Story more compassionate and more empowering, without denying difficulty. - Highlight one behavior change implied by the new story.

The Coach becomes a narrative editor who protects dignity.

AI-Directed Configure your Coach ›

Frame it as: “Train your AI to recognize your disempowering narratives.”

  • Feed the Coach 2–3 examples of your First Stories.
  • Ask it to name common phrases or assumptions that tend to show up.
  • Invite it to gently flag those patterns in future conversations and ask, “Do you want to try a Second Story here?”
6. The Constraint Inversion Engine
Constraint Flip

Uses limits and blockers as creativity fuel instead of kill switches.

Human Classic exercise ›
  • Write a single constraint (“We can’t use external AI tools,” “Our budget is tiny”).
  • Flip it: “What becomes uniquely possible because this constraint exists?”
  • Generate at least 5 ideas that only make sense under this constraint.
AI-Integrated Coach collaborates ›
Constraint Inversion: Constraint: "..." First attempt at flip: "..." Coach: - Improve my constraint-flip so it feels concrete, not fluffy - Generate 5–10 ideas that become attractive specifically because of this constraint

The Coach functions as an idea engine tuned to your reality.

AI-Directed Configure your Coach ›

Frame it as: “Give your AI this constraint so it learns how to innovate inside your boundaries.”

  • List your non-negotiables (compliance, budget, tools allowed).
  • Ask the Coach to treat these as guardrails, not annoyances.
  • Invite it to explicitly say “Because of your constraints X and Y, here’s a creative option.”
Stage 3

Clarify & Expand

Sandbox → Designer

Once people can play again, these muscles sharpen judgment, widen time horizons, and extract clarity from complex situations.

7. The Judgment Triad
Three-Lens Decision Check

Aligns surface facts, recurring patterns, and deep principles before a decision is made.

Human Classic exercise ›
  • Facts: List what is objectively true right now.
  • Patterns: Recall similar situations you’ve seen before.
  • Principles: Write 2–3 values or non-negotiables that should govern this decision.
  • Check for conflicts between the three; adjust the decision accordingly.
AI-Integrated Coach collaborates ›
Judgment Triad: Facts: "..." Patterns I've seen before: "..." Principles/values that matter here: "..." Coach: - Spot any contradictions or blind spots across the three lenses - Suggest 2–3 decision options that honor my principles while respecting the facts
AI-Directed Configure your Coach ›

Frame it as: “Feed your AI your decision logic so it can detect blind spots.”

  • Describe 1–2 past decisions you’re proud of and how the three lenses aligned.
  • Describe 1–2 decisions you regret and where the misalignment was.
  • Ask the Coach to watch for those misalignments in future reasoning.
8. The Temporal Lens Stack
Time-Horizon Shift

Evaluates decisions at three time horizons: 1 week, 6 months, 5 years.

Human Classic exercise ›
  • Take a decision or habit.
  • Ask: “What does this do to my life/work in 1 week? 6 months? 5 years?”
  • Notice where short-term and long-term trade off.
  • Adjust the decision to better respect the long-term identity you care about.
AI-Integrated Coach collaborates ›
Temporal Lens Stack: Decision or habit: "..." Coach: - Describe likely consequences in: - 1 week - 6 months - 5 years - Where do these conflict with the kind of person I say I want to become?
AI-Directed Configure your Coach ›

Frame it as: “Teach your AI how you weigh present vs future identity.”

  • Explain what you typically prioritize: immediate relief, medium-term stability, or long-term growth.
  • Tell the Coach what kind of gentle challenge you want when you sacrifice long-term for short-term too often.
  • Ask it to periodically ask, “How does this serve your 5-year self?”
9. The Rapid Clarity Protocol
30-Second Synthesis

Compresses noise into: What it’s about, what it means, and what it implies.

Human Classic exercise ›
  • Write messy notes about a situation or idea.
  • Then force a 3-line summary:
    • Anchor: What is this about?
    • Insight: What is the key thing to notice?
    • Implication: What follows from that?
AI-Integrated Coach collaborates ›
Rapid Clarity: Here are my messy notes: " ... paste notes ... " Coach: - Give me: - Anchor (1 sentence) - Insight (1–2 sentences) - Implication (1–2 sentences, focused on action or risk)
AI-Directed Configure your Coach ›

Frame it as: “Upload messy thinking; let the AI learn your synthesis pattern.”

  • Feed the Coach examples of problems plus the Anchor/Insight/Implication you eventually arrived at.
  • Ask it to mimic your style of synthesis.
  • Later, compare its summary with your own and refine the rules together.
Stage 4

Full Orchestration

Designer → Orchestrator

The capstone muscle integrates everything: context-setting, AI collaboration, synthesis, and decisive action.

10. The Intelligence Cycle
Orchestration Loop

A repeatable loop: context → intelligence → synthesis → action. This is the Orchestrator’s core operating system.

Human Classic exercise ›
  • Context: What am I actually trying to achieve?
  • Intelligence: What information or perspectives do I need?
  • Synthesis: What does all this mean when put together?
  • Action: What is the smallest meaningful next step?
  • Run this loop explicitly for any important project or decision.
AI-Integrated Coach collaborates ›
Intelligence Cycle: Context: "Here is what I'm trying to do..." Intelligence request: "Help me understand X / generate options for Y / pressure-test Z..." Coach: - Summarize 3–5 key insights from this exchange - Name one obvious but important next step - Name one non-obvious but promising experiment

The loop becomes a shared ritual between human and AI.

AI-Directed Configure your Coach ›

Frame it as: “Configure your AI to run this loop with you continuously.”

  • Describe your typical work context (roles, types of problems, constraints).
  • Explain how you like intelligence presented (bullets vs narrative, risks vs opportunities, etc.).
  • Tell the Coach what “synthesis” looks like when you feel satisfied.
  • Ask it to always close big exchanges with: “Here’s what this means, and here are your next two steps.”
